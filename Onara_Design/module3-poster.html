Personal Wellness Plan
Research-based goals for healthy eating, physical activity, and personal hygiene

Plan summary

This wellness plan uses current teen guidelines to set one realistic SMART goal for each area. The focus is consistency: eat better, move every day, and keep a simple hygiene routine that is easy to follow and easy to check.

S
Specific What exactly do you want to accomplish?
M
Measurable How will you track your progress and know you succeeded?
A
Attainable Is this realistic based on your current fitness level and habits?
R
Relevant Why does this goal matter to your health, confidence, or performance?
T
Time-bound What is your final deadline or end date?

Guideline snapshot

1
Healthy eatingFor a high-school teen: build meals with fruits, vegetables, whole grains, protein foods, and dairy. Limit added sugars, sodium, and saturated fat.
2
Physical activityFor teens ages 6 to 17: at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity each day, plus muscle and bone strengthening 3 days a week.
3
Personal hygieneWash hands with soap at key times, brush twice daily for 2 minutes, floss once a day, and keep a steady shower routine.
1

Healthy Eating

Current state

I eat some fruits and vegetables, but I still rely on snacks and sugary drinks sometimes.

SMART goal

For the next 4 weeks, I will build balanced meals using MyPlate at lunch and dinner, make half my plate fruits and vegetables, choose whole grains, and choose water instead of sugary drinks at least 5 days each week.

SSpecific: balanced lunch and dinner choices using MyPlate.
MMeasurable: at least 5 days each week on a food checklist.
AAttainable: starts with water, one snack swap, and better plate balance.
RRelevant: supports energy, focus, and long-term health.
TTime-bound: 4 weeks.

Action plan

Pack one fruit or veggie snack, drink water with meals, and choose lean protein and low-fat dairy when available.

Evaluate / reached when

I will use a daily food checklist and review it every Sunday. I will know I reached the goal when I meet the 5-days-per-week target for 4 full weeks.

2

Physical Activity

Current state

I stay active in class and sports, but I do not always move for a full hour.

SMART goal

For the next 4 weeks, I will get at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity every day and do muscle- and bone-strengthening activities 3 days each week.

SSpecific: daily movement plus strength and bone-building work.
MMeasurable: 60 minutes per day and 3 strength days weekly.
AAttainable: uses walking, sports practice, and short bodyweight sets.
RRelevant: improves fitness assessment performance and confidence.
TTime-bound: 4 weeks.

Action plan

Walk after school, practice a sport or workout, and add push-ups, squats, jump rope, or basketball on strength days.

Evaluate / reached when

I will track minutes on a weekly activity calendar. I will know I reached the goal when I complete 60 minutes of activity daily and strength work 3 days each week for 4 full weeks.

3

Personal Hygiene

Current state

I wash my hands and brush my teeth, but I can be more consistent with my routine.

SMART goal

For the next 4 weeks, I will wash my hands with soap and water at key times, brush twice a day for 2 minutes, floss once a day, and shower regularly.

SSpecific: handwashing, brushing, flossing, showering, and deodorant.
MMeasurable: nightly checklist for each hygiene habit.
AAttainable: keeps supplies ready and follows one repeatable routine.
RRelevant: supports health, confidence, and respect for shared spaces.
TTime-bound: 4 weeks.

Action plan

Keep soap, toothbrush, floss, and deodorant ready; wash after the bathroom, before eating, and after coughing or sneezing; shower after sports.

Evaluate / reached when

I will use a nightly checklist and make sure my hygiene supplies stay stocked. I will know I reached the goal when I complete the routine every day for 4 full weeks.
