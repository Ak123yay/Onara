# Ideal Customer Profile — Onara

_Who Onara is built for, their pain points, and who it's explicitly not for._

---

## Primary ICP: The "Invisible Local Business"

**Profile**:
- Single-location small business owner
- 1–10 employees
- $100K–$2M annual revenue
- Has a Google Business Profile listing (or wants one)
- No website, or has a website that's 5+ years old and not mobile-friendly
- Not technical — doesn't know what a CMS is
- Pays someone else to handle anything digital

**Industries** (in priority order based on Google Places data volume):
1. Restaurants / cafés / bakeries
2. Hair salons / barbershops / beauty
3. Plumbers / electricians / HVAC / contractors
4. Dentists / chiropractors / medical practices
5. Accountants / bookkeepers / tax preparers
6. Fitness / gyms / personal trainers
7. Auto repair / detailing
8. Real estate agents (individual, not brokerages)
9. Lawyers (solo practitioners)
10. Pet groomers / veterinarians

**Geographic focus** (launch):
- English-speaking markets: US, UK, Canada, Australia
- Urban and suburban — higher density of the above business types
- Rural markets: lower priority (fewer reviews, less GBP data)

## Pain Points

| Pain Point | How Onara Solves It |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "I don't have time to build a website" | 60–120 second generation |
| "I don't know how to update a website" | No updates needed — site reflects GBP data |
| "Web designers are too expensive ($500–$2000)" | $12/month, no setup fee |
| "I built a Wix site but it looks bad" | AI-generated, professional design |
| "My site isn't showing up on Google" | SEO-optimized HTML with structured data |
| "I don't know what to write on my website" | AI writes copy from GBP data |

## Decision Triggers

What pushes an ICP user from "aware" to "sign up today":
- Competitor just got a nice website
- Customer said "I couldn't find you online"
- Realtor/accountant/trade association recommended getting a website
- Just opened a new location or rebranded
- Just claimed their Google Business Profile for the first time
- Tried to build something on Wix and gave up after 2 hours

## Willingness to Pay (WTP)

**Current spend benchmarks** (what the ICP already pays):
- Existing website via a local web designer: $500–$2,000 one-time, $50–$150/month maintenance
- DIY on Wix / Squarespace: $16–$35/month (but requires 3–10 hours of setup and maintenance)
- GoDaddy "do it for me" service: $100–$200/month
- Nothing — no website at all (most common for contractors)

**Observed WTP for Onara**:
- Strong buy signal at < $15/month — aligns with "less than a cup of coffee a week" framing
- $12/month (Starter) is below the threshold where SMB owners question ROI
- $29/month (Pro) is justified when framed against a single new customer covering 1 year of cost
- Annual plan at $99/year ($8.25/month effective) increases conversion among cost-conscious owners

**Price sensitivity triggers**:
- SMB owners compare to what they currently pay a freelancer or designer, not to SaaS tools
- They do NOT compare to Wix — they compare to "hiring someone"
- Loss framing outperforms gain framing: "your live URL goes offline" > "upgrade for more features"
- Business owners who already spend on GBP ads ($100–$500/month) convert faster — they understand digital ROI

**Segment WTP table**:

| Segment | Current Spend | WTP (Onara) | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| No website, never tried | $0 | $12/mo | Needs proof via trial |
| Tried Wix, gave up | $0–$17/mo | $12/mo | Motivated by frustration |
| Has old freelancer site | $50–$150/mo | $29/mo | Replacing ongoing cost |
| Runs GBP ads | $100–$500/mo | $29/mo | Digital-savvy, ROI-aware |
| Agency-referred | $0 | $12–$29/mo | Trusts the recommender |

## Secondary ICP: The SMB-Adjacent Helper

- Accountants who refer their small business clients
- Trade association administrators managing member directories
- Business coaches who advise SMB owners
- Web designers who want to offer fast "starter sites" before their full project

These users may not use Onara directly but drive signups through referral. See `wiki/business/distribution.md` for referral programs.

---

## Explicit "Not For"

| Customer Type | Why Not |
|--------------|---------|
| E-commerce businesses | No product listings, no shopping cart |
| SaaS companies / tech startups | Need custom design and dynamic functionality |
| Large enterprises | Need brand compliance, CMS, and multi-user workflows |
| Power users who want to edit HTML | Onara is intentionally no-customization |
| Non-English speaking markets (v1) | Content generation is English-only at launch |
