# ADR-006: Why Reverse Trial over Freemium

**Date**: 2026-05-14  
**Status**: Accepted  
**Deciders**: Solo founder

---

## Context

Onara needs a go-to-market pricing model that maximizes trial-to-paid conversion while minimizing signup friction for non-technical SMB owners. The model must work with no marketing budget and a solo founder doing outbound.

## Decision

Use a **reverse trial**: 14 days of full Pro access, no credit card required. After 14 days, users are downgraded to a free tier (3 sites, 3 revisions/month) unless they choose a paid plan.

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Rejected Because |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Freemium** (free forever with limits) | 2–5% conversion; users get comfortable with free tier and never upgrade; subsidizing non-paying users indefinitely |
| **Credit-based** (e.g., 3 free generations, then pay) | SMB owners don't understand "credits"; feels like a trap; high early abandonment |
| **Trial with credit card required** | Removes the highest-converting SMB segment who distrust putting in a card before seeing value |
| **Direct paid only** (no trial)| High barrier for SMB owners who have been burned by Wix/Squarespace subscriptions they couldn't cancel |
| **7-day trial** | Not enough time for SMB owners to see ROI (they may generate a site on Day 1 but the "aha moment" of a customer finding them online takes days) |

## Reasoning

**Loss aversion beats feature unlocking**: Showing users the full Pro experience and then removing it is psychologically more powerful than never giving them access. "You're losing features you already use" converts better than "unlock these features."

**14 days is the right window**: Long enough for a customer to find them online (Days 1–7), show their site to friends/family (Days 7–10), and feel the loss of full access at Day 14. Short enough to create urgency.

**No card removes the #1 SMB barrier**: Local business owners have been burned by subscriptions they couldn't cancel. "No credit card" is a trust signal that says we're confident in the product.

**Conversion benchmarks**: Reverse trials convert at 4–12% vs freemium 2–5% (Product-Led Growth Institute). At 1,000 signups, that's 40–120 paid users vs 20–50. The difference is material at bootstrap scale.

**Still have a free tier**: Downgraded users keep 3 sites and 3 revisions/month — enough to stay engaged and potentially upgrade later. This is better than hard-paywalling and alienating the user.

## Trade-offs Accepted

- Engineering complexity: pg_cron required for Day 11/13/14 emails and Day 14 downgrade
- Customer support load: some users confused by downgrade (mitigated by clear email sequence)
- Free tier subsidy: downgraded users still consume some server resources (low at v1 scale)

## Consequences

- `subscriptions.plan` starts as `trial`, transitions to `free` on Day 14 if no upgrade
- 3 Resend emails triggered by pg_cron (Day 11, Day 13, Day 14)
- Free tier enforces 3-site and 3-revision limits in API routes
- All `FEATURE_*` flags default to the Pro behavior during trial (no artificial limits)
